The Rolling
Spring Roll

The Truck

The Rolling Spring Roll began when aspiring Vietnamese chef, Joe Bui decided to introduce the delicious and healthy food he grew up with to Long Island. Uncertain where he would find the best market for his cuisine, Joe started his business from a food truck. He opened for business on March 5, 2012 on Spagnoli Road in Melville, NY.

The Name

Many names were considered from his friends and family, but Joe decided he wanted to highlight the most famous of his mother’s recipes, the spring roll - a crunchy, finger-sized pork spring roll enhanced by vermicelli noodles, carrots and several Vietnamese spices. Since it would be served out of a truck, Joe anticipated he would be “rolling” around Long Island and the name “The Rolling Spring Roll” was born.

The Building

The success of The Rolling Spring Roll on Spagnoli Road was so great, that Joe never left that location. His approach to serve clean, healthy food – made fresh from scratch, gave his customers a delicious dining experience with unique flavors. After over a year of serving people on Spagnoli Road, Joe decided to open a full service restaurant. The Rolling Spring Roll, the FIRST Vietnamese restaurant on Long Island, opened on Main Street in Farmingdale on July 31, 2013.
